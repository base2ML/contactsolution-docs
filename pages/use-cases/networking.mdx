# Networking

Make every connection count with digital contact cards.

## Why Digital Cards?

### The Problem with Paper Cards
- Lost or forgotten in pockets
- Information becomes outdated
- No way to track engagement
- Environmental waste
- Manual data entry required

### The Digital Advantage
- Always accessible on your phone
- Update once, syncs everywhere
- Track who viewed your card
- Integrate with your CRM
- Instant contact saving

## Networking Scenarios

### Conference Networking

**Before the Event:**
1. Update your card with relevant role/company
2. Add social links for your industry
3. Download your QR code for quick access

**At the Event:**
1. Open your wallet pass
2. Let contacts scan your QR code
3. Card saves to their contacts instantly

**After the Event:**
1. Check your analytics to see who engaged
2. Export contacts to your CRM
3. Send personalized follow-ups

## Optimizing Your Card

### Profile Photo

A professional headshot increases engagement by 3x:
- Clear, recent photo
- Good lighting
- Professional attire
- Simple background

### Key Information

Essential fields for networking:
- **Name** - How you want to be remembered
- **Title** - Your current role
- **Company** - Where you work
- **Email** - Professional email
- **LinkedIn** - For professional connections

### Social Links

Choose strategically:
- **LinkedIn** - Almost always include
- **Twitter/X** - If you're active professionally
- **GitHub** - For developers
- **Website** - If you have a portfolio

## Analytics for Networking

### What to Track

**View Count:**
See how many people viewed your card after an event.

**Device Type:**
Mobile views = in-person scans
Desktop views = email/link clicks

**Time of View:**
Peak engagement times help you understand when to follow up.

### Acting on Analytics

**High Views, Low Saves:**
Consider simplifying your card or adding more compelling info.

**Late Night Views:**
People are researching you - ensure your LinkedIn is current.

**Geographic Patterns:**
If you see views from unexpected locations, you may have viral sharing.

## Best Practices

### Do
- Keep your card updated with current info
- Check analytics after events
- Follow up within 24 hours
- Use wallet passes for quick access
- Add card to email signature

### Don't
- Overload with social links
- Use outdated photos
- Forget to update job changes
- Ignore analytics insights
- Make card too long
